Power Quality Analysis

Energy wastage is significant and most often undetected with aging equipments, faulty terminations, poor installations, poor earthing, and unbalanced loads. Power quality analysis aims to identify such issues, avoid unplanned outages, avoid production interruptions and maintain an ideal plant performance.

Drawing poor quality of power, causes overheating of neutrals, overheating of transformers or motors, nuisance tripping of circuit breakers, blown fuses and eventual breakdown of heavy machineries, which are highly unpredictable and unsafe.

Some of the most common indicators to measure power quality:

Harmonic distortions, identifying various harmonic frequencies and total harmonic distortions are critical to identify quality of power.

Transient distortions or voltage surges are particularly dangerous causing irreversible damage to equipment, common issues are lightning strikes and insulation failures.

Voltage sags and swells with distortion of 10% or more from normal voltage levels causes contactor chattering, plc failures, variable speed drives to fail

Voltage and current unbalances leads to challenges in maintaining balanced load when single phase loads cycle go on/off during peak hours. This causes unavoidable unablances in voltage and current leading to high current consumption which eventually causes overheating of equipments

Power Quality standards such as IEEE 519, IEC 61000 -4-30, EN 50160, IEC 61000 -2-4 are essential in monitoring power quality. Identifying faults, its location, its time, its duration, its cause and frequency of such faults could provide insights on how to mitigate repetition and arrest such faults from happening again. This ensures asset equipment protection for longer productivity with zero interruptions. Resulting in a predictive and preventive maintenance operations.
